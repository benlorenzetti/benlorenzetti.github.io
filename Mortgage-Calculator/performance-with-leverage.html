Leveraged Performance

To compare the value of nominal equity buildup (Σn) with potentially negative monthly cash flows, we need to sum monthly cash flow with weighting factors for opportunity cost (ω) of other investments.

From the price floor model, the mortgage-free cash-flow of a property (q) is expected to grow with an underlying appreciation factor (π), while the nominal mortgage payment (p) remains fixed. Furthermore, unlike equity, cash-flow dividends can be invested elsewhere (one month later) for a some average monthly return (Ω) with monthly return factor ω=1+Ω. We define Q'n as the sum of all these nominal ('unadjusted') gains over months 1 to n of a mortgage.

Q'n= ∑m=1 n ( qπm-p ) ωm-1

Adjusting this sum of cash-flow returns by an inflation factor (π) and scaling out the mortgage payment makes the cash dividend independent of mortgage size and introduces the mortgage-owner preference factor (c) into our equations.

Qnp = π-n ∑m=1 n ( c-1 πm-1 ) ωm-1 where c=pq = Vmarket Vfloor

Applying the geometric series and normalizing to obtain a closed form solution::

Qnp = π-n [ πc (πω)n -1 πω-1 - ωn-1 ω-1 ]
Qnp = π-n [ πc (πω)n πω-1 + c(πω-1)-Ω c(πω-1)Ω - ωn Ω ] for Ω=ω-1
Qnp = πc ωn πω-1 + πω-1- Ω/c (πω-1)Ω π-n - ρn Ω for ρ=ωπ

Next for Ω=ω-1 and Π=π-1 we make an approximation

πω-1= (Π+1) (Ω+1)-1 = ΠΩ+Π+Ω ≈Π+Ω

to avoid introducing a new variable to the summed dividend gain:

Qnp ≈ πc ωn Ω+Π + Ω+Π- Ω/c Ω(Ω+Π) π-n - ρn Ω
Qnp ≈ πc ωn Ω+Π + 1+ΠΩ -c-1 Ω+Π π-n - ρn Ω

Finally we rewrite the inflation-adjusted, normalized cash-flow gain after n months in a form that will be more convenience later (for Maclaurin series approximations).

Qnp ≈ ( πc-1 1+Π/Ω ) ωnΩ + ( 1+ΠΩ -c-1 1+Ω/Π ) π-nΠ - ( ΡΩ ) ρnΡ for Π=π-1 Ω=ω-1 Ρ=ρ-1 and ρ=ω/π

For consistent comparisons, we keep the mortgage-free cash flow (q) constant in terms of dollars at the beginning of the loan period such that inflation appears as a deflation of the burden of the inital monthly payment (p).

The fractional performance over n months from purchase to sale can be found from the sum of cash gains and mortgage equity gain, adjusted for inflation. Both are relative to the inital down payment (E0) and buyer's fee (F1), which do not need to be adjusted for inflation because we adjust the other terms to the value of money at the beginning of the loan period.

yn = ∑m=1 n (q-p π-m ) ωm + π-n Σn + E0 -F2 E0 +F1

Using constant dollars allows us make a simplification right off the bat, by treating your future selling fee as equivalent to those of the person selling us the property now, we are able to think about the cost of both fees, the buyer's fee (F1) and seller's fee (F2) as single overall cost.

E0 -F2 E0 +F1 = E0- F2+ F1+ F2- ( F1+ F2) E0 +F1 = 1- F1+ F2 E0 +F1

The second simplification is to normalize the function to be independent of property value and loan size. We define the effective leverage ratio L= p×RN E0 +F1 as the inital loan size relative to the initial investment (down payment plus buyer's fees).

yn = 1+ LRN ( ∑m=1 n (qp- π-m ) ωm + Σnp π-n - F1 +F2 p )

Third, we define a bargain factor (c) comparing the affordability of renting vs buying and also an underwater time (T) before a purchase is likely have positive performance, yielding

yn = 1+ LRN ( ∑m=1 n ( c-1 - π-m ) ωm + ∑np -T )

with c=pq mortgage preference factor, T= F1 +F2 p time underwater and L= p×RN E0 +F1 effective leverage ratio.

Fourth the performance of an investment is often reported differentially (think percentage gain) rather than fractionally by subtracting one. We will use both forms going forward.

Gn = LRN ( ∑m=1 n ( c-1- π-m ) ωm + Σnp π-n -T )

Finally we replace the geometric series with closed forms, just as was done to find RN.

Gn= LRN ( ωc ( ωn-1 ω-1 ) - ωπ ( (ωπ) n -1 (ωπ) -1 ) + Σnp π-n -T ) for ω>π≥1

This result is the second of a series calculating the opportunity cost of not buying your house to better understand the mortgage preference scalar c. The previous result found the nominal equity buildup (Σn) in a mortgage and the next analysis will evaluate the effects of investment period (n) on overall yield (y).
