Price to Earnings Ratio

The price to earnings ratio (P/E) is a commonly used measure of stock market prices. Another way this information is reported is earnings per share (EPS) together with the stock share price.

The goal of the mortage calculator is comparing real estate investments to stocks. The graph below shows the results of the real estate model over the last 40 years compared to the S&P 500. For more information on yellow and red curves, they are is the culmination of the mortgage calculator series.

Assumptions

There are three anecdotal assumptions noted on the graph and a normalization scalar c' (the same as in the P/R ratio). All of these could be more tightly quantified with more data by professional researchers, economists, or companies. Nonetheless, the results seem pretty well correlated and believable in the magnitude of differences: family home ownership returns seem to have been more than twice (2.75x) the stock market historically. About 25% of this seems to be due to active property management vs. passive stock holding and the other 75% due to the use of debt.

Code

The MATLAB (or Octave) script peratio.m used to make this plot is available to my readers as a tool, free under an MIT license. It relies on functions from my data scripting library. Up to date data is available at the sources cited below.

Hashed Freddie Mac Data

Single family mortgage data is publicly available from Freddie Mac under limited terms and conditions. For research purposes, running the model with the Freddie Mac data produces similar results with fewer assumptions. These results are hashed onto the plot above.
